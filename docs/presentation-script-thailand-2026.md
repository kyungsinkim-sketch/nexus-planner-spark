# Re-Be.io — Presentation Script
## Thailand Ad Festival, March 2026
### Speaker: Kyungsin (Pablo) Kim
### Duration: 17-18 min (+ video)

---

## [SLIDE 1 — Black screen, single quote]
### ~1 min

Intelligence is not about what you know.

It is about how successfully you interact with the world.

*(pause)*

Think about that for a moment.

In our industry — the creative industry — better intelligence doesn't mean more knowledge. It means better interaction. Better decisions with clients. Better negotiations with collaborators. Better judgment under pressure.

And yet — how much of that interaction do we actually remember?

---

## [SLIDE 2 — Personal Story]
### ~2.5 min

Let me be honest with you.

I've been in this industry for over fifteen years. I've led pitches. I've won some. I've lost many. And when I look back, the wins were never about having a better idea on day one.

To win competitive pitches consistently, I needed years of painful feedback. Failed bids that taught me what clients actually cared about — versus what I *thought* they cared about.

Building the right team took trial and error. Dozens of projects where the chemistry was wrong, where the structure didn't fit the brief, where I learned — the hard way — who works well with whom, and why.

Even budgeting. Knowing how to build an efficient production budget isn't something they teach you. It takes project after project of over-spending, under-estimating, and slowly developing a feel for what's real and what's optimistic.

And — this might sound trivial — but even finding good restaurants for client dinners? That required eating at *a lot* of bad ones first.

*(pause for laughter)*

The point is: every good decision I make today is built on a foundation of failures, corrections, and accumulated experience.

But here's the problem.

Most of that experience? It's in my head. And some of it — the most valuable parts — I've already forgotten.

The decision I made in 2021 that saved a project? I can barely remember the details. The negotiation pattern that worked with a specific client type? Gone. The mistake I swore I'd never repeat? I repeated it.

I realized: the core of better decision-making isn't more knowledge. It's making sure our failures, our interactions, our judgment — become a database. A structured memory that helps us evolve our thinking.

That's exactly why we built Re-Be.

## [SLIDE 3 — The Problem]
### ~3 min

Let me show you what I mean.

*(SLIDE: "Where does experience live?")*

Every creative project is shaped by hundreds of micro-decisions. Not just the big creative idea — but: Which vendor do we use? Do we push back on the client's timeline? Do we sacrifice scope or quality? How do we handle this conflict between the director and the producer?

These decisions happen across Slack. WhatsApp. Email. Notion. Phone calls. Zoom meetings.

And then they disappear.

*(SLIDE: Three scenarios)*

**Scenario one.**

Your best producer leaves the company. She spent three years building relationships with clients, learning their patterns, knowing exactly when to push and when to compromise. All of that knowledge — that judgment — was in her head. And in ten thousand Slack messages nobody will ever search through.

She walks out the door, and three years of accumulated intelligence walks out with her.

**Scenario two.**

You're working with the same client for the third time. And for the third time, the project stalls after the first creative review. Two-week delay. Every time.

The pattern is right there — in last year's meeting recordings, in email threads, in chat discussions. But nobody connects the dots because the information is scattered across five different tools.

**Scenario three.**

A junior creative joins your team. Talented. Eager. But how do you transfer ten years of judgment? You can write a manual — but manuals describe *what* to do. They never capture *why*.

Why did we choose this director over that one? Why did we push back on the budget here but not there? Why did we change the concept after the second client meeting?

The "why" lives in conversations. And conversations disappear.

*(pause)*

This is not a knowledge problem.

This is a memory problem.

---

## [SLIDE 4 — Category Creation]
### ~2 min

*(SLIDE: Three layers)*

Now, AI has been evolving rapidly. But let's look at *what* it's been solving.

The first wave — **Knowledge AI** — focused on documents. Structuring information. Making it searchable. Tools like enterprise search engines that help you find the right file, the right answer, the right policy.

The second wave — **Automation AI** — focused on operational data. Contracts, tables, workflows. Making execution faster and more reliable.

Both are valuable. Both are necessary.

But both miss something critical.

They capture what's written down. They don't capture what's *discussed*. They process documents. They don't process *decisions*.

*(SLIDE: "The Missing Layer")*

Modern creative work doesn't run on documents alone. It runs on:

Decisions. Trade-offs. Alignment. Creative negotiation. Judgment.

And none of that is structured. None of that is owned. None of that becomes memory.

Until now.

---

## [SLIDE 5 — The Solution]
### ~3 min

*(SLIDE: "Re-Be.io — The Collaborative Experience Layer")*

Re-Be.io captures what other tools miss: the *collaborative experience*.

Not just what your team did — but why they decided to do it.

Here's how it works.

*(SLIDE: "Record")*

**First — Record.**

Re-Be connects to the tools your team already uses. Meetings. Chats. Emails. And it doesn't just store the raw data — it structures it.

When a meeting ends, Re-Be automatically extracts: Who made which decision. What alternatives were considered. Why one option was chosen over another. Who was involved.

Conversation becomes structured memory.

Not a transcript you'll never read — but a decision record you can learn from.

*(SLIDE: "Reflect")*

**Second — Reflect.**

After three projects, five projects, ten projects — patterns start to emerge.

"Client revisions after the first creative review consistently delay launch by two weeks."

"Budget cuts always follow late-stage direction changes."

"This particular client rejects the first concept every single time."

Your AI agent begins to see what humans forget. Not because it's smarter — but because it remembers everything, and connects the dots across time.

*(SLIDE: "Realize Better")*

**Third — Realize Better.**

This is where it gets powerful.

Re-Be moves from reflection to anticipation. Your personal AI agent starts to suggest:

"This pattern appeared in your last three projects. Should we add a buffer before the client review?"

"The last two times you worked with this vendor, delivery was late. Consider an alternative."

"Your team's decision-making slows down after the third revision round. Might be time to align now."

Now AI is no longer retrieving knowledge.

It is helping you make better decisions — *before* problems occur.

Record. Reflect. Realize Better.

## [SLIDE 7 — Ownership]
### ~2.5 min

*(SLIDE: "Who owns this intelligence?")*

Now — there's one more thing I want to talk about. And I think it might be the most important part.

Let's go back to scenario one. Your best producer leaves the company.

In today's world, when she leaves — her email gets deleted. Her Slack history becomes inaccessible. Her meeting recordings are archived somewhere nobody will ever look.

But what about her *judgment*? The way she learned to negotiate with difficult clients. The instinct that told her to push back on a bad brief. The pattern recognition she built over years of experience.

Should that belong to the company? Or should it belong to *her*?

*(pause)*

We believe it should belong to her.

Re-Be separates two kinds of intelligence:

**Organizational context** — project details, client information, team structures. This stays with the company.

**Personal experience** — your decision patterns, your learning, your instincts, your growth. This belongs to you.

Your personal experience is stored in a RAG — a Retrieval-Augmented Generation layer — that you own. Not inside a company tool. Inside your own identity layer. A digital wallet for your professional intelligence.

When you move to a new company, your organizational access ends. But your accumulated wisdom comes with you. Your AI agent continues to grow, continues to learn, continues to help you make better decisions — across your entire career, not just your current job.

This is not just data portability.

This is **experience sovereignty**.
